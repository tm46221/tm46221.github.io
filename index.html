A History of the Miami Dolphins

The Past

The Dolphins joined the NFL in 1970, after the merger of the AFL and the NFL. Two short years later, they had a perfect season, the first and only ever to happen in the modern NFL. It sounds like this franchise would be in for a storied record of many successes after the early found success in this new NFL; however, you would be wrong. While they did go on to win another Super Bowl the next year, the success has yet to be repeated as they haven't won a Super Bowl title since. They went to two more after this one with one of, if not the greatest quarterback of all time in Dan Marino. Unfortunately, they did not win either. And since that 1985 Super Bowl, they haven't been back to one since. After the great head coach, Don Shula, the Dolphins have gone through head coaches like crazy. As a result of this, some bad coach named Nick Saban picked Daunte Culpepper over future Hall of Famer, Drew Brees, and as a result, set the franchise back many years.

The Present

The current coach of the Miami Dolphins is Adam Gase. He is the former offensive coordinator of the Chicago Bears. He is regarded as a quarterback whisperer and has brought out the best of quarterback Ryan Tannehill. Unfortunately, due to injuries, Gase has yet to have a full season with Tannehill; however, in games that he has him healthy, the Dolphins are 11-7. This is reason to see promise in Gase's coaching ability because without Gase, Tannehill is 29-35. The rest of the offense struggles, but this is not including the many injuries they suffer, and the lack of stability at the quarterback position. His success has earned him one playoff berth in his two years as head coach in which he clinched with backup quarterback Matt Moore. The other season was lackluster due to the poor play of quarterback Jay Cutler. Gase has brought all his players together, built a strong locker room, and certified that he is the right man to lead this offense forward into the future.
